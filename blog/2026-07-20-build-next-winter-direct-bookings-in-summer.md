## The Busiest Season Is Your Best Data Season

In July and August your property is full. Rooms sell themselves, the front desk is stretched, and direct booking strategy feels like a problem for the quiet months. That instinct is exactly backwards. The peak weeks are when the largest number of real, paying, high intent guests pass through your doors. What you learn about them now is what fills your rooms when demand falls away in November.

Every summer guest is a future direct booking waiting to happen. The question is whether you are capturing enough about them to bring them back without paying commission a second time.

## What Walks Out of the Door at Checkout

Consider a 60 room property running at 90 per cent occupancy through August. That is more than 1,600 guest stays in a single month. If half of those arrived through an OTA, you hold almost nothing about 800 guests: no verified email, no stay preferences, no reason to contact them again. The OTA owns that relationship, and it will happily resell those same guests back to you next year.

The hotels that grow direct revenue treat the summer rush as a data capture window. They make sure that every stay, whatever channel it came through, ends with the property owning the guest relationship rather than renting it.

## Three Moves to Make Before the Rush Ends

### Capture a verified email at check in, every time

An OTA booking gives you a masked address that disappears the moment the guest leaves. A short check in step that confirms a real contact email, framed around the digital key, the wifi login or the booking confirmation, converts an anonymous OTA guest into someone you can reach directly for years.

### Record one preference per guest

You do not need a full profile. One useful field, a room type, a special occasion, a dietary note, an arrival reason, is enough to personalise the next message. A guest who celebrated an anniversary with you in August is a near certain direct booking next August if you remember and reach out.

### Tag the channel they arrived through

Knowing which summer guests came via OTA tells you exactly who to win over to direct. These are the guests worth a tailored autumn offer, because converting them once removes a commission you would otherwise pay on every future stay.

## From Summer Stay to Winter Booking

The mechanism that turns this data into revenue is a simple post stay sequence, set up now and running automatically into the low season:

| Timing | Message | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 2 days after checkout | Thank you and feedback request | Capture sentiment, surface issues privately |
| 4 to 6 weeks later | Personalised return offer | Convert the OTA guest to a direct booker |
| Ahead of the quiet season | Exclusive direct rate or package | Fill rooms when demand is soft |

A flow like this typically recovers a meaningful share of guests who would otherwise default back to an OTA for their next trip. The cost is close to nothing. The margin difference between a commission free direct booking and a repeat OTA booking is the entire point.

This is the same principle behind treating guest data as an owned asset rather than a transaction record, a theme we covered in [first party data is the new loyalty](/blog/first-party-data-is-the-new-loyalty). Summer is simply when you have the most of it flowing through the building.

## Why This Cannot Wait for September

If you start thinking about winter occupancy in October, the richest data source of the year has already left. The guests are gone, the emails were never captured, and you are back to buying demand through paid channels and OTAs at full cost.
