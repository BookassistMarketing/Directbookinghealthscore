## Sell the Dream While the Dream Is in Front of Them

In high summer your property is at the peak of its appeal. The terrace is full, the pool is busy, the restaurant is humming, and every guest is having exactly the experience your marketing tries to capture all year. This is the single best moment to sell a gift voucher, because the desire to return is at its strongest and the proof of why is all around them.

A gift voucher is direct revenue you collect today for a stay the guest will take later, very often in your quietest weeks. It is one of the simplest tools a hotel has for smoothing seasonality, and most properties barely use it.

## Why Vouchers Are a Seasonality Tool, Not Just a Gift

Hotel revenue rises and falls with the calendar. Vouchers let you move money against that curve. Cash arrives in the strong months, when guests are inspired and willing to spend. Redemption tends to land in the soft months, when a voucher holder books a stay you would otherwise have struggled to sell.

There is a second quiet advantage. A meaningful share of vouchers are never redeemed at all, or are redeemed for less than their face value. That breakage is pure margin. Even setting it aside, a voucher sold direct carries no OTA commission, captures the buyer's contact details, and frequently brings a brand new guest into your property, because vouchers are so often bought as gifts for someone else.

## The Three Audiences Buying in Summer

**The current guest.** Someone who is enjoying their stay right now is primed to buy a return visit or to gift one. A simple prompt at checkout, or a tasteful card in the room, turns a happy moment into a future booking.

**The gift buyer.** Vouchers are a natural present for birthdays, anniversaries, weddings and the festive season ahead. A guest who loved their summer stay is an ideal advocate to buy your hotel as a gift for friends and family, introducing you to people who have never stayed before.

**The lapsed guest.** A late summer voucher campaign to past guests, framed around a treat for the darker months, reactivates relationships that would otherwise go cold until next year.

## Making It Work in Practice

The mechanics matter less than the timing, but a few choices lift results:

| Choice | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| When to promote | Peak weeks, when satisfaction and footfall are highest |
| What to sell | Stay vouchers and experience vouchers, not only fixed cash amounts |
| Where to surface it | Booking confirmation, in room, checkout, and a dedicated site page |
| Validity | Long enough to drive off season redemption, with clear terms |
| Channel | Direct on your own site, so you keep the full value and the data |

The key is to treat vouchers as a planned campaign tied to the season, not a dusty link in the website footer. Sold deliberately during the summer peak, they become a reliable cash injection for the quiet months and a steady source of commission free direct guests.

This sits alongside the wider goal of owning the guest relationship rather than renting it from an OTA, the same thinking behind [why most hotels are losing revenue to OTAs](/blog/why-hotels-lose-money-to-otas). A voucher is simply that relationship, paid for in advance.
